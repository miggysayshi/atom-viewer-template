The Trade in Motion

First hour looked promising. Price ticked up to +1.86 at 10:55 — about 66% of my stop distance in my favour — before momentum stalled. Through the lunch hour the tape turned two-sided, and by 13:40 price had ground all the way back to -2.80, tagging the stop level without triggering it (intraday wick, not a close).

From there, the action was uninspiring. No follow-through either direction — the kind of afternoon that grinds a position. With the planned target still $8.40 away and the time window shrinking, the EOD exit was the right call.

Exit

Closed at 15:55 at $729.08 for a final P&L of -$0.58 (-0.21R). Position size was 100 shares, so the dollar hit was small but the lesson is real: the breakout was real, the follow-through wasn't.

✦
Lesson Trail after 1R. Once the trade reached +1.86 (close to 1R of progress), a tighter stop at breakeven would have turned a scratch into a small win. The MFE/MAE ratio of 0.66 flagged that the trade was struggling well before EOD — earlier management could have locked in a flat result.
